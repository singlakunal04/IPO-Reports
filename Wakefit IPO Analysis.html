Investment Strengths | Key Bullish Factors

1. Profitability Inflection

Turned profitable in H1FY26 with 14.3% EBITDA margin and ₹3.56 Cr PAT after 3 years of losses. Shows operational leverage is real.

2. Strong Revenue Growth

25.2% CAGR (FY23-25) demonstrates robust demand for D2C home solutions. Outpacing organized peers.

3. Vertical Integration

5 modern manufacturing facilities with automation eliminate middlemen, reduce costs, and enable margin control—sustainable competitive advantage.

4. Omnichannel Mix

65% from own channels (website + 125 COCO stores) provides pricing power, brand control, and margin buffer vs. pure online or distributor models.

5. Broad Product Portfolio

3,000+ SKUs across mattresses (60%), furniture (29%), furnishings (11%) reduce revenue concentration and enable cross-selling to 2M+ customer base.

6. Growing Repeat Customer Base

36% repeat buyers with 2M+ total customers demonstrates brand loyalty and lower customer acquisition costs over time.

✓ Growth Story: Wakefit demonstrates characteristics of a sustainable growth company—profitability inflection, operational leverage, and brand scale.

Investment Risks | Key Bearish Factors

1. Premium Valuation

At 4.7x EV/Revenue, Wakefit trades at 130% premium to Sheela Foam despite 2.3x smaller revenue. High growth already priced in.

2. Recent Profitability

Only H1FY26 profitable after FY23-25 losses. H1FY26 PAT margin of 4.9% vs 14.3% EBITDA margin suggests elevated D&A and finance costs.

3. COCO Store Expansion Risk

Plan to add 117 stores by 2028. Offline margins lower than online. Execution risk, payback uncertainty in competitive market.

4. Channel Concentration

65% revenue from own channels. Website stagnated at ₹350-400 Cr/year. Cyberattacks, downtime, or reduced footfall risk would materially hurt sales.

5. Cyclical Demand Risk

Mattresses purchased every 10-15 years. Furniture & furnishings are discretionary—vulnerable to economic slowdowns.

6. Inventory & Working Capital

Inventory rose 60% in H1FY26 (₹260 Cr from ₹164 Cr). Working capital management challenges ahead as store base expands.

⚠️ Valuation Risk: At current prices, investor expectations are very high. Any slowdown in profitability growth could trigger significant correction (see Stanley Lifestyles, down 50% YoY).

Investment Recommendation & Conclusion
HOLD / WAIT-AND-WATCH

Not Recommended for Fresh IPO Application at Current Valuation
Consider Post-Listing Opportunities After Price Correction

Why NOT Buy at IPO?

  • Valuation Premium: 4.7x EV/Revenue vs 1.8x for Sheela Foam
  • New to Profitability: Only H1FY26 profitable—track record of 2+ quarters needed
  • Execution Risk: Aggressive 117-store expansion plan untested
  • D2C Correction Risk: Post-IPO D2C stocks (Nykaa, Mamaearth) typically correct 30-50% within 6 months
  • Market Timing: Better entry points likely post-listing

Investor Guidance

  • Retail/Small Investors: Skip IPO. Wait 3-6 months for post-listing correction
  • HNI/Institutions: Buy at 15-20% discount post-listing if profitability sustains
  • Long-term Bullish: Strong 25%+ CAGR, vertical integration, and omnichannel advantage support 5-year bull case
  • Key Monitoring: Q1FY26 results, COCO store unit economics, inventory management post-IPO

Summary: Wakefit is a growth story with real operational leverage, but current valuation doesn't offer sufficient margin of safety. Better risk-reward post-correction.
